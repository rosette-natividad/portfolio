Safe Munchies

Information Technology Solution

☆ ★ ☆ ★ ☆

Chow Chums was a group of nine members for our IT Solution project in my Management of Technological Organizations course in Fall 2023, during which we designed an app called Safe Munchies. This project spanned the entire semester and involved four stages.
Goal

The objective of the project was to develop and design an IT solution to a problem. Our focus area was healthcare. We came up with a specific problem rationale after one of our members talked about how he is allergic to eggs. Ultimately, we chose to solve the inconveniences that people with food allergies and dietary restrictions face when going out to eat. We developed a prototype app, conducted research and interviews, and took note of our progress using a Gantt chart.

Roles

Each member had a designated role, with some sharing the same roles, each with their own corresponding tasks. The roles are as follows:

  • Team Leader
  • Project Manager
  • Documentation Manager
  • Business Analyst and Context Modeling (2)
  • Market Researcher (2)
  • Prototype Developer (2)

I had the role of Prototype Developer which I shared with another member. We worked together to create the Safe Munchies app but also had other duties to balance out the team’s workload. We brainstormed ideas for the app and I designed it and made it functional using Figma. I also used Adobe Illustrator to design the logo of the app. See prototype screenshots here.

The Pitch
"With Safe Munchies, you can be safe and sound with the best snacks around."

Each year in the US, it is estimated that severe allergic reactions affect about 90,000 Americans a year. The app shows restaurants in the area of Rutgers and filters through their menus to find items the user can eat. Safe Munchies works via compiling data from online spreadsheets of ingredients in menu items and filtering it based on the needs of the user. It’s a low cost option considering RU express already lists restaurants in the area, this would just be a step up in helping those with food allergies and intolerances. It would be a showcase of Rutgers commitment to accessibility for their students, allowing those with dietary restrictions the ability to enjoy the restaurants around campus with their friends without feeling excluded.

Stakeholders & Customers

Our stakeholders are RU (Rutgers University) dining services. They manage the university's restaurants and dining halls, so they are the most suitable organization to engage with regarding this creative app idea. Another key stakeholder is the restaurants that surround the Rutgers area.

The customers are first-year Rutgers students due to dining plan requirements and others with nutritional disabilities.

We believe that they will have open ears in regard to our pitch and in making this app a success. As well as the customers will have high interesting paying for our app
