StudyMate

Mobile Application

☆ ★ ☆ ★ ☆

StudyMate was a mobile application group project in my Human Computer Interaction course of Fall 2023. The group for the project was called HCI Innovators and consisted of five members.
Goal

Our goal was to develop a platform specifically designed for college students, facilitating task management and time organization for optimum efficiency, with the aim of helping students enhance productivity and reduce academic stress.

Problem

People's work efficiency and time management often suffer due to constant distractions. Many college students struggle to effectively manage their workload, often due to limited time or competing priorities in their lives.

Context

College students frequently juggle many courses, assignments, and extracurricular activities in today’s fast-paced academic environment, making efficient time management critical. The rise of digital distractions, like social networking and entertainment, has made it even more difficult to stay focused on assignments. Furthermore, the transition to online learning as a result of the COVID-19 pandemic has blurred the lines between personal and academic life, making it more difficult to keep a disciplined schedule. To address these difficulties, a tool that supports college students in effectively managing their time and avoiding distractions is required to boost their productivity and academic performance.

Proposed Solution

To address the problem of college students struggling with time management and distractions, we propose the development of a mobile app called 'StudyMate.' StudyMate is a comprehensive platform designed specifically for college students to enhance productivity, improve time management, and reduce academic stress. The app will provide a range of features to help students stay organized, focused, and on top of their academic responsibilities.

App Vs. Website

In terms of our platform, both a mobile app and a web app would be efficient, considering that more people carry cell phones with them more often than laptops. However, many students also complete assignments on their laptops. Nevertheless, for the sake of simplicity, our prototype will focus on creating our platform through a mobile app.

Data Collection
Gathering Data

Our data collection plan involves interviews and a survey to gain insights into students' task prioritization, work-life balance, and reasons for procrastination. The interview covers three topics, including task prioritization, work-life balance, and reasons for procrastination, with five questions each. The survey consists of 15 questions, addressing employment status, screen time, weekly free time, class workload perceptions, health issues, work organization methods, productivity, stress levels, and difficulty remembering deadlines. The approach aims to provide a thorough understanding of students' academic experiences and challenges.


Analyzing Data

In these interviews, students consistently prioritize classes based on major or personal interest, with the difficulty of the subject and relevance to future goals influencing their choices. Balancing academic commitments and free time poses challenges for many, prompting the use of various strategies like time management and setting boundaries. Procrastination emerges as a shared obstacle, negatively impacting academic performance through missed deadlines and increased stress. Canvas is basically the only way for them to track their assignments. Personal values and long-term goals play a crucial role in shaping individuals' approaches to studies and work-life balance. Seeking help and maintaining a diverse set of coping strategies are recognized as important factors in managing workload and overcoming challenges. Overall, while experiences vary, these interviews showcase common themes in students' academic journeys.

The Prototype

I was tasked with creating the prototype of StudyMate and chose to use Figma to develop it. Below is the design of our mobile application. Based on the responses from our questionnaires, we decided to make it an app that would request access to Canvas (a popular Learning Management System for colleges and universities). How is this different from other productivity apps? We emphasize social interactions. Social media is very prevalent in young adults. We planned to make social interactions compatible with all institutions. Users could add college students from different institutions as “friends” which opens up several many social interactions to occur between them. While the app itself will send notification reminders to complete assignments, friends can also trigger notification reminders. A reminder from a friend can be more meaningful than an automatic reminder.
